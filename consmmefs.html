Overview

Turning complexity
into clarity.

CONSMMEFS (Cooperative Society Management & Member Financial Services) needed a powerful administrative dashboard that could handle multi-layered financial workflows without overwhelming the administrators who had to use it daily.

The challenge: consolidate multiple disconnected tools into one interface, for users ranging from tech-savvy admins to cooperative officers with minimal digital experience.

The Problem

Three tools for
one workflow.

Administrators were using a spreadsheet for member records, a separate web app for loan approvals, and a third-party accounting tool for financial reporting. Completing a single workflow — reviewing a loan application — required context-switching between all three.

This wasn't just inefficient — it introduced errors. Data entered in one tool often became stale or inconsistent in another. Staff were maintaining duplicate records and manually reconciling discrepancies every week.

The Core Insight

"Every task required three tabs. They weren't using a dashboard — they were conducting an orchestra."

Research & Discovery

Mapping the
real workflow.

I started with task analysis sessions — watching administrators complete their most frequent tasks without any guidance, noting every context switch, every hesitation, every workaround. Then I ran workshops with the team to map the ideal workflow from their perspective.

01
Task Analysis

Observed 5 administrators completing their top 10 weekly tasks. Documented every tool-switch, every error, every manual workaround they'd normalized without realizing.

02
Information Architecture Workshop

Ran a card sorting session with 4 administrators to understand how they mentally categorized different types of data and workflows — which revealed a mismatch with how the existing tools were organized.

03
Progressive Disclosure Mapping

Built a complexity map — identifying which features were needed daily vs. monthly vs. annually — to determine what to surface prominently and what to tuck into secondary navigation.

Design Decisions

Before &
after.

The redesign wasn't about adding features — it was about removing friction from the ones that already existed, and bringing them together in a way that matched how administrators actually thought about their work.

Before

3 separate tools for one complete task

Manually reconciled data weekly

New staff needed 2 weeks to onboard

Reports generated manually in Excel

No audit trail for approvals

After

Single unified dashboard for all tasks

Real-time data sync across all modules

New staff self-onboarded in 2 hours

Automated reports generated on demand

Full approval audit trail built in

Outcome

When design gets
out of the way.

The unified dashboard eliminated the need for multiple tools. Task completion time dropped significantly, and the biggest indicator of success: new staff members self-onboarded without a single training session — meaning the mental models in the design matched how people naturally thought about cooperative financial management.

The automated reporting module alone saved the finance team an estimated 6 hours per week that had previously been spent manually exporting data to Excel.

Success Metric

"The best interface is the one no one notices. When users stop talking about the tool and start talking about the work, you've done your job."
